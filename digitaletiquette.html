10 Examples of bad etiquette

Despite Virginia Shea’s work in 1994, there are still plenty of examples of bad digital etiquette to this day and the first one is “Typing in CAPS!” words in capital letters appear bolder and "louder" than mixed case, and this is sometimes referred to as "screaming" or "shouting". The second one is “spam:” according to (Tolentino, 2015) spamming refers to irrelevant or unsolicited messages sent over the Internet. Spam is bad for several reasons. It is annoying, it wastes time, and it misuses resources. The third thing is “use of offensive language”: use of bad words provokes a physical stress response, not only that but, writing bad things on the internet will affect you in the future, next time you swear on Twitter, just remember that future employers are watching.

Steal other people’s identity: When someone steals your identity, they pretend to you, with this they can do numerous things in your name like committing any forms of fraud, stalking and targeting other people, and many more. The other issue is the “distribution of illegal material”. Piracy undermines and steals from the people who created that content and not only that, but this also has a negative impact on the economy, it reduces sales in music/movie industries. The other thing is “expecting a response straight away” By all means, if you want to text back straight away, go for it. But this is why it may be better to wait. In our brains, a text back from that special someone is considered a reward. Replying too soon (although great at first) over time can cause the other person to start to see you as less value

People should avoid broadcast only, sometimes people should engage with others, every time an individual interacts with you on social media, overall awareness of your brand increases, according to (CareMalta, 2018) engaging with others enhances Mental Health. And the most annoying one is people who ask a question that can be found on the site or on Google (Melin, 2013), I do not get it. Why can’t people do some simple Internet research instead of asking stupid questions?
